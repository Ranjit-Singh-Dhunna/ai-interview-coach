## Detailed Feedback:

### Verbal Communication:
The candidate should focus on improving their verbal communication skills during interviews. This includes speaking clearly, slowly, and concisely to ensure that responses are easily understood by interviewers. Additionally, practicing common interview questions and scenarios can help the candidate become more comfortable with verbalizing thoughts and ideas in a structured manner.

### Problem-Solving Demonstration:
To better showcase problem-solving abilities during interviews, candidates should prepare examples of complex challenges they've faced in past projects and discuss the specific steps they took to overcome them. This can include identifying the problem, researching solutions, implementing a solution, testing it, and iterating as needed. By providing concrete examples, candidates can demonstrate their critical thinking skills, adaptability, and ability to learn from experience.

### Career Goals Clarity:
Candidates should tailor their career goals to each job they apply for, emphasizing how the position aligns with their long-term aspirations and personal growth. In this case, the candidate could have provided more detail about their career aspirations within the specific domain (e.g., software development, data science, or product management) and discussed how the role being interviewed for supports those goals. This demonstrates a clear understanding of the position's requirements and shows that the candidate is serious about their professional development.

### General Feedback:
Overall, the candidate has a strong technical background and relevant project experience. However, they need to work on their verbal communication skills during interviews and provide more specific examples when discussing problem-solving or career goals to showcase their abilities effectively. With continued practice and refinement, the candidate can improve their interview performance and increase their chances of securing their desired role.
